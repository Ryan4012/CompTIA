## Notes:

**The CIA Triad -**
- Confidentiality: Ensures unauthorized users are unable to access to sensitive information.
- Integrity: Ensures there is no unauthorized modification to information, systems, and controls (intentionally/unintentionally)
- Availability: Ensures information is readily available as needed upon request

**The DAD Tirad -**
- Disclosure: Exposure of sensitive information to unauthorized parties. Also known as data loss.
- Alteration: Unauthorized modification of data that results in a variation of interpretation
- Denial: Disruption of legitimate access to information

**Risk Categories -**
1. Financial Risk
2. Reputational Risk
3. Strategic Risk
4. Operational Risk
5. Compliance Risk


**Financial Risk -**
Financial Risk can consist of both direct & indirect monetary damage to an organization.

Direct Costs:
- Equipment
- Contractor Servicies

Indirect Costs:
- Sensitive data on missing device
- Stolen plans produced by computer

**Reputation Risk -**
Negative publicity incurred through announcement of data breach can directly influence the reputation of an organization. This can be quanified through loss of customers, employess, and stakeholders.

An organization may find difficulty recovering in the court of public opinion.

**Strategic Risk -**
A long-term impact risk who's goal is to hinder an organization's major milestones/performance. Events such as product launches & first to market strategies are strategic risks that can bleed into impacting future opportunities.

**Operational Risk -**
Unlike Strategic Risk, Operational Risk attacks the organization's ability to perform day to day task. Efforts to slow down business processes, delay delivery of orders, or increasing the time for production can impact an organization's obligations to its stakeholders.

What's the difference? Operational Risk is impactin short-term objectives while Strategic Risk impacts long term, major goals.


**Compliance Risk -**
Organizations under legal or regulatory requirements may face additional scruntiny due to a data breach. Hospitals, dental clinics, and urgent cares fall under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) in which Protected Health Information (PHI) is required to be covered under the CIA Triad. Similarly, educational institutions such as school districts, college/universities, and transcript vendors are impacted through the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) in which Personally Identifiable Information (PII) & academic records are sealed to unauthorized individuals.

Data breaches can result in additional sanctions & fines for breach of compliance.


**Gap Analysis -**
Is an audit or review of an organization's security controls. Gaps should be treaded as potential risks and remediated based on severity. 

**Security Control Categories -**
1. Technical - Digital enforcement of the CIA Triad
2. Physical - Security controls impacting the physical world
3. Operational - Processes to monitor and manage technology
4. Managerial - Administrative procedural mechanics of risk management

**Security Control Types -**
1. Preventative - Stops an incident before it occurs
2. Deterrent - Discourages intruder from attempting to violate policy
3. Detective - Identify incidents that have already occurred
4. Corrective - Remediate issues from past incidents
5. Compensating - Mitigates risks associated with exceptions to a policy (Risk Acceptance)
6. Directive - Informs stakeholders of what they can/can't do
